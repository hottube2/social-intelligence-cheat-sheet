# PDF Layout Guide

## Recommended page order
1. Cover page
2. Short introduction
3. How to use this cheat sheet
4. Section 1 — 10 phrases to start conversations
5. Section 2 — 10 phrases to sound more confident
6. Section 3 — 10 polite ways to say no
7. Section 4 — 10 phrases to negotiate calmly
8. Section 5 — 10 phrases to handle awkward situations
9. Closing page

## Visual direction
- Minimal and premium
- White background with dark text
- One accent color only
- Strong spacing
- No cluttered illustrations
- Mobile-readable typography

## Typography suggestions
- Title: Inter SemiBold or Playfair Display + Inter pair
- Body: Inter, Source Sans 3, or Helvetica
- Section heads: 20 to 28 pt
- Body text: 11 to 12 pt for print, 13 to 15 pt for digital-first layouts
- Phrase cards: 12 to 14 pt with strong line spacing

## Layout guidance
- Wide margins for breathing room
- Use numbered phrase cards or short rows
- Keep one section per page or spread if possible
- Add subtle divider lines between phrases
- Make the file easy to skim on a phone
- Avoid dense paragraphs

## Canva implementation idea
- Cover page with title, subtitle, and small trust line
- One intro page
- One how-to-use page
- One page per phrase section
- One closing page with a short encouragement line

## Web-to-PDF export idea
You can also render the product as an HTML page and print to PDF. If you do this:
- Use a fixed content width
- Avoid very large hero blocks
- Use explicit page breaks between sections
- Keep backgrounds simple for clean printing
