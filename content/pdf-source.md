# Social Intelligence Cheat Sheet

## Introduction
Most people do not struggle because they have nothing to say. They struggle because the right words do not come fast enough in the moment. This cheat sheet is designed to remove that friction. Each line is short, practical, and easy to adapt so you can speak with more clarity and less stress.

## How to Use This Cheat Sheet
1. Read through each section once to get familiar with the phrasing.
2. Pick 3 to 5 lines that feel natural in your voice.
3. Practice them out loud so they are easier to recall under pressure.
4. Use the phrases as templates, not scripts. Adjust them to fit the moment.
5. Revisit the guide before meetings, dates, calls, or difficult conversations.

## Section 1 — 10 Phrases to Start Conversations
1. How’s your day going so far?
2. What’s been the most interesting part of your week?
3. How do you know the host?
4. What are you working on lately?
5. I like your perspective on that. How did you get into it?
6. What brought you here today?
7. What do you usually enjoy doing outside of work?
8. That sounds interesting. Tell me more.
9. I’ve heard a little about that, but not enough. What’s it really like?
10. What’s something good you’ve been focused on recently?

## Section 2 — 10 Phrases to Sound More Confident
1. Here’s what I think makes the most sense.
2. I’m comfortable with that decision.
3. Let’s keep this simple.
4. I’d prefer to handle it this way.
5. That doesn’t work for me.
6. I’m clear on what I need.
7. I’m happy to discuss it, but I want to be direct.
8. That’s my position at this point.
9. I trust my judgment on this.
10. I’m not available for that, but I can suggest another option.

## Section 3 — 10 Polite Ways to Say No
1. I appreciate it, but I’m going to pass.
2. Thanks for thinking of me, but I can’t commit to that.
3. I’m not able to take that on right now.
4. That won’t work for me, but I appreciate the invite.
5. I need to say no so I don’t overextend myself.
6. I’m keeping my schedule tight right now.
7. I can’t do that, but I hope it goes well.
8. I’m not the right person for this one.
9. I have to decline, but thank you for asking.
10. I’m going to leave that there for now.

## Section 4 — 10 Phrases to Negotiate Calmly
1. Can we look at a version that works better for both sides?
2. I’d be more comfortable at this number.
3. What flexibility is there on the terms?
4. Let’s focus on the outcome we both want.
5. I’m open to moving forward if we can close this gap.
6. What would make this workable on your end?
7. That’s higher than I expected. Can we revisit it?
8. I’d need a bit more value to say yes to that.
9. Can we slow it down and look at the details?
10. I want to be fair, but I also need this to make sense.

## Section 5 — 10 Phrases to Handle Awkward Situations
1. Let’s reset for a second.
2. I don’t think that came out the way I meant it.
3. No worries, these moments happen.
4. Let’s not make it bigger than it needs to be.
5. I hear what you’re saying.
6. We may be reading this differently.
7. Let’s move forward from here.
8. That was a little awkward, but we’re okay.
9. I’d rather be clear than leave it weird.
10. Let’s keep this simple and direct.

## Closing
Clear communication is rarely about having perfect words. It is about having useful words ready when the moment arrives. Keep this guide close, use what fits, and let repetition turn confidence into habit.
